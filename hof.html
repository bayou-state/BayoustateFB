Bayou State Football Hall of Fame

Honoring the Players Who Built the Standard

Legacy Era Inductees

Terrance Blanchard – RB (2017–2020)

Program’s first true offensive star. Set multiple rushing records and carried Bayou State during its early competitive years.

Andre LeBlanc – DE (2016–2019)

Defensive anchor of the late 2010s. Career sacks leader and first nationally recognized defensive player in program history.

Marcus Duhon – WR (2024–2027)

Elite route runner and record-setting receiver who helped modernize the passing game during the mid-2020s resurgence.

Daniel Ricks – QB (2026–2029)

All-time passing leader who elevated the offense to new heights and set multiple school career records.

Corey Mitchell – S (2023–2026)

Ball-hawking safety who anchored the secondary and set the career interceptions record.

2031 Playoff Run Inductees

Jose Dwyer – CB (2028–2031)

Lockdown cornerback during Bayou State’s first College Football Playoff appearance in 2031. Rarely targeted, eliminated opposing WR1s, and set the tone for the program’s defensive identity.

Captain Wilds – CB (2029–2031)

CB1 during the historic 2031 playoff run. Played opposite Dwyer and formed one of the most feared corner duos in school history. Vocal leader and defensive captain.

Juan Rabe – EDGE (2028–2031)

Defensive game-wrecker who carried the defense throughout the 2031 playoff run. Dominant pass rusher and emotional heartbeat of the unit.
